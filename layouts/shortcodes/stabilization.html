Three regimes of stabilization

The same fifteen‑node reactive graph in each scope: three sources (diamonds) at the base, three effects (squares) at the fringe. Source values are incremented; the panels show only how staleness spreads and resolves — not data. What differs between them is purely when the recompute is scheduled, and whether a moment exists in which the graph is current but no effect has yet touched the world.
